About Me

In all my 17 years of life, growing up immersed in both Korean and American cultures has given me a unique perspective on the cultural relativity in healthcare systems and their impact on people’s lives. These experiences have inspired me to pursue nursing, not just to follow procedures but build a bridge between worlds of caregiving. Outside of this goal, I’m an artist and a cheerleader, and I love finding creative ways to express myself and connect with others. Whether it’s through painting, where I can bring my ideas to life with color, or through cheerleading, where I can inspire and lift the spirits of others, I aim to make a positive impact. Ultimately, I believe that both in healthcare and in life, the small moments of care and connection are what truly make a difference. As I continue my journey, I’m committed to blending my passion for science with my creativity, working towards a future where I can advocate for those in need while inspiring others to smile along the way.

Technology for Good

Color of Ours

Color of Ours is a web-based project that raises awareness of color blindness through interactive learning and creative giving. Using Python and OpenCV, I developed image-processing algorithms that simulate how color-blind individuals perceive images across three major types—Protanopia, Deuteranopia, and Tritanopia. The website allows users to explore these differences visually and purchase original artwork, with all proceeds directed toward funding special-lens glasses for those affected by color blindness.

Interdisciplinary Research

Project 3Sigma — The Primate Portal

Project 3Sigma was a Carnegie Mellon University capstone collaboration with the Primate Portal Lab and Seneca Park Zoo, exploring the origins and nature of thought through technology-assisted learning. Our multi-disciplinary team of 26 students and 19 mentors worked across psychology, app development, game design, robotics, and data science to support the training of Olive Baboons solving cognitive tasks via digital interfaces.
