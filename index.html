About Me

These experiences have inspired me to pursue nursing, not just to follow procedures but build a bridge between worlds of caregiving.

Technology for Good

Color of Ours

Color of Ours is a web-based project that raises awareness of color blindness through interactive learning and creative giving. Using Python and OpenCV, I developed image-processing algorithms that simulate how color-blind individuals perceive images across three major types—Protanopia, Deuteranopia, and Tritanopia. The website allows users to explore these differences visually and purchase original artwork, with all proceeds directed toward funding special-lens glasses for those affected by color blindness.

Research Internship

3Sigma with Carnegie Mellon University

Project 3Sigma was a capstone collaboration at Carnegie Mellon University with the Primate Portal Lab and Seneca Park Zoo, exploring the origins and nature of thought through technology-assisted learning. Our multi-disciplinary team of 26 students and 19 mentors worked across psychology, app development, game design, robotics, and data science to support the training of Olive Baboons solving cognitive tasks via digital interfaces.
